# Research Backlog

Purpose: capture **unanswered questions** that emerge from outreach and
conversations, so they can be investigated later *if and when evidence makes
them relevant*.

This is a passive backlog. Rules:
- Record questions only. Do **not** answer them yet.
- Do **not** perform deep research yet.
- No solutions, recommendations, feature proposals, architecture work, or
  implementation plans.
- When a conversation reveals a recurring challenge, that topic may be promoted
  from here into focused research. Until then: evidence-collection mode.

Status legend per item: `open` (captured, uninvestigated).

## Questions surfaced by Conversation #1 (Adam Elijilah, resident perspective — 2026-07-14)

Captured as open questions only; not answered, not generalized. Adam is one
resident. These become real research topics only if later conversations show a
recurring pattern.

- [open] When a communication outage hits, what is the actual sequence by which a
  family loses access to aid-distribution information (which channel fails first,
  and what is the fallback)?
- [open] How do beneficiaries currently receive aid/info directly to their phones
  (WhatsApp / Telegram / Facebook) when connectivity is intermittent — what is the
  real delivery rate during outages?
- [open] When digital payments / transfers become unavailable during an outage,
  what do affected people actually use instead, and who coordinates that?
- [open] Are there existing community practices for re-sharing aid information
  offline (word-of-mouth, printed, forwarded caches) once the primary channel
  drops?
- [open] What referral pathways exist between residents and people in humanitarian
  work (Adam mentioned possible referrals) — how do those connections form?
