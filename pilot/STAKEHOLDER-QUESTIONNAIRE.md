# Nidaa — Stakeholder Questionnaire (written brief)

**Purpose:** collect evidence to *falsify* Nidaa's assumptions — not testimonials.
Send this instead of (or before) a call. A 15-minute call is optional, only if
something in your reply surprises us and we want to probe.

**How to answer:** specifics beat opinions. Name the tool, the incident, the
date, the cost. You do not need to answer every question. Skip what doesn't
apply. Corrections and "you're asking the wrong question" are the most useful
responses.

---

## 1. Your context
- What is your role, and what does a typical coordination day look like?
- What does "coordination" mean in your work — what are you actually trying to make happen?

## 2. The last time it broke
- Describe the most recent time coordination failed (or was painful). When? What happened? What was the cost?
- Was the failure about: information (you didn't know what existed) · matching (help existed but couldn't be connected) · verification (you couldn't trust what you heard) · logistics · supply · access/security · trust · something else?

## 3. Current tools
- What do you use today (WhatsApp, Telegram, Airtable, Sheets, radio, paper, other)?
- What works about them that you'd never give up?
- What specifically breaks — and when (always, or only under load / disconnection)?
- What have you tried and abandoned, and why?

## 4. Connectivity
- When the internet drops, what breaks first, and for how long?
- Can your team still post and read needs/offers with NO internet for days? How do you manage that now?
- Is "works offline" a deciding factor, or a nice-to-have you'd trade for something else?

## 5. Trust & verification
- When someone posts "we need X at location Y," how do you decide whether to believe it today?
- Who do the people you serve actually TRUST to confirm something is real (a local committee, a known org, a UN body, nobody)?
- If a trusted party marked entries "verified," who would that be in your context — and why would people accept them?
- What would have to go wrong for a verification system to do harm (compromised verifier, board used as a targeting list)?

## 6. The hard one
- Be blunt: is coordination even a priority problem for you, or is it dwarfed by resources / access / security?
- If you could fix ONE thing about coordination, what would it be?
- What would make a NEW coordination tool worth adopting over what you use now?
- What question should we be asking that we didn't?

## 7. (Optional) On Nidaa specifically
- We've built a prototype: offline-first board, posts saved on the device, trusted verifiers mark entries. In what recent real incident would it have helped — and in what incidents would it have done nothing?
- If Nidaa is solving the wrong problem, what's the right one?

---

Return by reply, or as a doc. No format required. Honesty over polish.
