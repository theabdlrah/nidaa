# Nidaa — Learning Plan (replaces "build toward pilot")

This plan governs the outreach phase. It is subordinate to
`ASSUMPTION-TRACKER.md`, which is the **source of truth**. Enthusiasm, effort
invested, features built, and time spent do NOT determine the next move.
**Evidence does.**

## Reframed success

Success is **not** obtaining a pilot at all costs. Success is **reducing
uncertainty enough to make a high-confidence decision.**

The Day 90 goal is an **evidence-based decision**, not "first pilot."

Possible outcomes (the correct one is whichever the evidence supports):

1. Proceed to pilot.
2. Narrow scope.
3. Pivot.
4. Stop.

---

## Milestones

### Day 30
- 5–10 problem-discovery conversations completed.
- Assumption tracker populated with **real evidence** (no testimonials).
- Initial assessment of **A1** (coordination is a priority), **A2**
  (information/matching bottleneck), and **A5** (existing tools insufficient
  for some communities).

### Day 60
- Patterns identified across conversations.
- Core assumptions classified as strengthened / weakened / falsified.
- Preliminary recommendation: proceed, narrow, pivot, or stop.

### Day 90
- **Formal decision point.**

---

## Decision framework

### PROCEED TO PILOT if:
- A1 (coordination is a priority problem) is supported.
- A2 (information/matching is a meaningful bottleneck) is supported.
- A5 (existing tools are insufficient for at least some communities) is supported.
- At least one community expresses genuine interest in testing an alternative.

### NARROW if:
- The problem exists but only for a specific segment, geography, or use case.

### PIVOT if:
- The pain is real but our proposed solution addresses the wrong layer.

### STOP if:
- Multiple conversations strongly falsify A1, A2, or A5.

---

## Operating rules
- Every conversation updates `ASSUMPTION-TRACKER.md` (template in that file)
  BEFORE next steps are discussed.
- No "they liked it" summaries. Record evidence that moved an assumption.
- The Pivot/Stop conditions are a **hard gate**, not a suggestion. Falsification
  is a successful outcome — it saves resources.
- Day 30/60/90 are checkpoints on the tracker, not deadlines to force a pilot.
