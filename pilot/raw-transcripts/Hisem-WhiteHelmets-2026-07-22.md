"Salam

Based on my experience in Syria, community-led coordination initiatives often remain alongside the formal humanitarian coordination system rather than becoming fully integrated into it. In practice, humanitarian actors usually engage with them mainly for the distribution of assistance, because these local initiatives and community actors have a much better understanding of the local context, community needs, and the people who are most vulnerable.

This cooperation can continue throughout the crisis, particularly as long as humanitarian needs remain. However, these initiatives do not always become part of the formal coordination structures. Their role is often more practical and operational, especially in identifying needs and facilitating the delivery of assistance, rather than in strategic decision-making or formal humanitarian coordination.

From my observation, their involvement may continue until the crisis or emergency phase comes to an end, although the level of engagement can vary depending on the context, available resources, and the relationship with formal humanitarian actors."
