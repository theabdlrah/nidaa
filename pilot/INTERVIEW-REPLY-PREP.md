# Nidaa — If Someone Replies Today: Be Ready

**Goal:** if any of the 7 Contacted (or a next-wave target) replies, we can move to a
real conversation immediately — no scramble. Everything below is pre-written.

## Opening (reply within 24h, keep it light)
> Thanks for the reply. I'm just after your perspective — I'm trying to falsify my
> own assumptions about coordination in low-connectivity settings, not sell anything.
> Whatever works for you: a 20-min call, or just reply here with whatever's true.
> No prep needed.

## Follow-up questions (pick 3–5; lead with their world, not our product)
1. Walk me through your role — what does a typical coordination day look like?
2. When coordination last failed, what specifically went wrong, and what did it cost?
3. **(PUBLISHING STEP — the one to push on)** Who actually posts coordination/aid
   info in your setup — a named role, a rotating volunteer, or nobody in particular?
   When a distribution point opens or a road closes, how does that fact get from the
   person who knows to the people who need it?
4. When the internet drops, what breaks first — and what do you do for coordination
   with NO connectivity for days?
5. When someone posts "we need X at Y," how do you decide whether to believe it today?
   Who would your community accept as the person who confirms it's real?
6. Blunt one: is coordination even a priority problem for you, or is it dwarfed by
   supply / access / security? If you could fix ONE thing, what?

## Assumption mapping (map their answers → tracker)
- "coordination isn't the real problem" → A1 weakens.
- "we couldn't find / connect known help" → A2 strengthens.
- "tools die when the network drops" → A3 strengthens.
- "we rely on [person/body] to confirm things" → A4/A6 strengthened (name the verifier).
- "WhatsApp is fine / good enough" → A5 weakens.
- "nobody owns posting / it's ad hoc" → A7 risk UP (publishing-step failure).
- "our info officer handles posting" → A7 risk DOWN.

## After the call (mandatory, before next steps)
1. Write the 3 bullets (falsified / confirmed / most surprising).
2. Create `pilot/assumption-log/<ORG>-<DATE>.md` from the template in
   ASSUMPTION-TRACKER.md.
3. Update ASSUMPTION-TRACKER.md (Evidence For/Against, Status, Confidence, Pivot Rule).
4. Update ASSUMPTION-NEXT-TESTER.md (evidence + next tester).
5. Move the contact to `Interviewed` in OUTREACH-LEDGER.md.
6. Regenerate journal (Build Log entry) + commit.
7. Do NOT log the reply as evidence until the conversation actually happened. A reply
   alone is not evidence.
