# Nidaa — Offline-First Community Coordination for Low-Connectivity Crisis Environments

## What is it?
Nidaa (نداء, "call/appeal") is an offline-first coordination platform that lets a
community share, verify, and discover local needs and resources — even when the
internet is slow, expensive, or cut off entirely. The phone is the source of
truth: a post is saved on-device first and synced only when a connection returns,
so nothing is lost when the network drops.

## What problem does it solve?
Under crisis, vital local information (where aid is, which clinics are open, who
needs what) gets scattered across chat groups and lost when connectivity fails.
There is no durable, searchable, trustworthy record a community can rely on.
Nidaa is that record.

## What makes it different?
- **Works offline** — usable with zero connectivity after first visit.
- **Local-first** — no data loss on disconnect; automatic retry sync.
- **Searchable** — persistent, filterable needs/offers, not vanishing messages.
- **Verifiable** — trusted verifiers mark entries confirmed; unverified stays visibly unconfirmed.
- **Arabic-first** — RTL built in from the start, not translated later.
- **No lock-in** — open source; your data is exportable; you can self-host.

## What are we asking?
A **4–8 week pilot** with approximately **30–100 participants** from your network,
using Nidaa for real coordination. We configure the instance with your data, train
your coordinators (under one hour total), and co-write a short field report with you.

## Time required?
**Less than one hour of training** for your coordinators. Posting takes seconds.
We handle setup, seeding, and support.

## What it is NOT
Not a chat app (keep WhatsApp — Nidaa can receive posts from it and broadcast its
link through it). Not a national information system. Not a surveillance tool — no
mandatory personal data, no third-party analytics.
