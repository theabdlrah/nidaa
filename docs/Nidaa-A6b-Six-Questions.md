# Nidaa — The Six-Question Story (the A6b live gate)

This is the ONLY script that can close G1/G2. The board's survival hinges on a
real coordinator answering these honestly. Send as a conversation, not a survey.
No pitch, no demo, no ask beyond 15-30 min of their perspective.

OPENING (say this first, verbatim-ish):
"I'm Abdul Rahman, a student researching how aid coordination actually works in
connectivity-stressed places like Gaza. I'm NOT pitching a tool — I'm trying to
find out whether the coordination problems I think exist are real, or whether
I've got the wrong end of the stick. I'd rather be corrected than build the
wrong thing."

THE SIX QUESTIONS:
1. Walk me through how your team coordinates needs and offers TODAY (WhatsApp,
   Telegram, sheets, radio, paper, word-of-mouth?).
2. What specifically breaks when connectivity drops or gets shut off?
3. Who actually owns the coordination — i.e. who is accountable when it fails?
4. Tell me about a time coordination WORKED well despite the constraints. What
   made it hold together?   -> this is G1 (success case)
5. Tell me about a time coordination COLLAPSED or information got lost. What
   broke first?             -> this is G2 (collapse case)
6. If a board existed that stayed usable offline and let trusted coordinators
   mark things "verified," would it slot into how you already work — or would
   it be one more thing to maintain? (Be brutal.)

CLOSE:
"Honest answers are the only useful kind. If my assumptions are wrong, tell me
which. No ask beyond this conversation."
