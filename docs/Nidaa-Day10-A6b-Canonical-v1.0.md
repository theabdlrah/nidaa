   trusted informal networks become important for obtaining, verifying, and sharing

  -> A DERIVED DESIGN/ADOPTION claim about Nidaa's survival, applying a moderator variable.

W-C — A6b Resolution Campaign (conditional split) [pilot/A6B-RESOLUTION-CAMPAIGN.md L149-156]

      "A loose coordinator / mutual-aid chat with no designated owner or mandate will

  - A6b-strong (owner/mandate present):
      "A coordinator with a designated owner, training/mandate, and low-burden capture

2. HISTORICAL A6b WORDING(S)
==================================================================

Chronological emergence (reconstructed from artifact dates):
- W-A (Day 6, 2026-07-18): the ORIGINAL project hypothesis — informal networks become
  important when formal coordination is stressed. This is what "A6b" meant at launch.
- W-D + W-C (Day 5 campaign, logged 2026-07-17; briefed Day 6): the hypothesis was
  sharpened into the conditional owner/mandate split (W-C) after public evidence showed a
  population split (White Helmets structure; ERRs chat-default). W-D is the one-line summary.
- W-B (Evidence Ledger, stamped Day 6 but reflects post-campaign reasoning): reframed A6b
  as Nidaa's product-survival condition. This is the most recent wording and is the one the
  Ledger labels "canonical" — but it is a design implication, not the tested hypothesis.

So the historical drift is: mechanism claim (W-A) -> conditional evidence claim (W-C) ->
product-survival claim (W-B). The three are related but are NOT the same statement.

==================================================================
3. DIFFERENCES BETWEEN WORDINGS
==================================================================

- W-A vs W-C: W-A answers WHY informal networks matter (formal overload -> informal fills
  gap). W-C answers WHETHER informal coordinators maintain structure and UNDER WHAT
  CONDITIONS (owner/mandate moderator). W-A is broader and untested by the six-question
  gate; W-C is the precise, gradable claim the gate validates. Conflating them is the core
  drift: winning W-C does NOT validate W-A.

- W-B vs W-C: W-B is a CONCLUSION drawn FROM W-C's moderator variable, applied to Nidaa's
  adoption. W-B says "the board dies without owner/low-burden/embed." That is a product
  implication of W-C, not W-C itself. Labeling W-B "canonical A6b" misassigns the label:
  the canonical TESTABLE A6b is W-C, not W-B.

- W-D vs W-C: W-D is the behavioural prediction implied by W-C (chat/committee default;
  documentation is a gap). Consistent with W-C; not a separate hypothesis.

- UNRECONCILED tension: W-A (informal fills formal gaps) is partly challenged by OQ-2
  evidence (formal + informal COEXIST; formal systems can hold — e.g. Aaron's platform,
  Uganda Health Cluster). W-A has NOT been re-examined against OQ-2. The Day-6 brief still
  carries W-A as the live assumption while the campaign operates on W-C. They must be
  explicitly separated, which v1.0 does below.

4. RECOMMENDED CANONICAL A6b STATEMENT v1.0
==================================================================

A6b (testable hypothesis, CANONICAL):
  "Whether grassroots / informal coordinators maintain structured operational information
   during active coordination and under pressure is CONDITIONAL on a designated owner/mandate
   and low-burden capture. Where an owner/mandate + low-burden capture exists, structured
   maintenance holds (Reality A, conditional). Where it does not, coordination defaults to
   chat/verbal and structured records are either a standing gap or collapse under pressure
   (Reality B)."

Companion statements (kept SEPARATE, not conflated with A6b):
- A6b-mechanism (W-A, SEPARATE hypothesis, NOT validated by the gate):
  "Trusted informal networks become important specifically when formal coordination is
   overloaded / delayed / unavailable." Status: supported only by n=1 practitioner
   corroboration (Aaron) + Solnit literature; challenged by OQ-2 coexistence evidence;
   open.
- A6b-design-implication (W-B, DERIVED): Nidaa survives only if it reaches a coordinator
  with ownership + low onboarding + workflow embed. This follows from A6b; it is not the
  hypothesis under test.

Why this canonical: it is the statement the six-question gate can actually confirm or
falsify; it preserves the empirically-sharpened moderator variable; it stops the project
from claiming W-A is validated when only W-C is.

==================================================================
5. SUCCESS CRITERION (for A6b itself)
==================================================================

A6b (conditional) is considered EVIDENCE-RESOLVED (not "validated" in the strong sense)
when at least ONE first-hand coordinator story via the six-question gate specifies:
  (i)   whether a designated owner/mandate existed,
  (ii)  what happened to structured info under a concrete pressure event, and
  (iii) lets us classify Reality A or B AND the owner/mandate condition,
from the ACTUAL Nidaa population (connectivity-stressed, protracted crisis, grassroots/
informal — not only semi-formal orgs). Either Reality A or B from a real coordinator is a
valid resolution of "what happens"; the milestone is the story, not a report.

NOTE: A6b-strong is already supported at the SEMI-FORMAL tier (White Helmets, Mones/MSF).
The remaining live gap is the LOOSE-INFORMAL tier (A6b-weak branch): one loose-informal
coordinator story (success OR collapse).

==================================================================
6. FALSIFICATION CRITERION
==================================================================

A6b-strong is falsified by a first-hand account where a coordinator HAD a clear owner/mandate
+ low-burden capture yet structured info STILL collapsed under pressure (moderator fails).

A6b-weak ("no owner -> no structure") is falsified by a first-hand account where a genuinely
loose, owner-less mutual-aid coordinator SPONTANEOUSLY maintained a structured register under
pressure.

A6b-mechanism (W-A) is challenged/falsified by sustained practitioner evidence that formal +
informal COEXIST and informal adds no unique value during overload, or that informal networks
themselves cause the failures (a G2 case). OQ-2 carries the stronger disconfirming pressure
already.
